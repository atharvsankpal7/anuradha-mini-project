Wastewater Treatment Plant

Introduction

Wastewater treatment is a process used to remove contaminants from wastewater or sewage and convert it into an effluent that can be returned to the water cycle. This process is essential for maintaining a clean environment and protecting public health.

Why is Wastewater Treatment Important?

Untreated wastewater can be harmful to the environment and human health. It can pollute rivers, lakes, and oceans, making them unsuitable for drinking, fishing, or recreation.
